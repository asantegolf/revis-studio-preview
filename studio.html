A gemologist’s eye,
brought to wool.

Revis Studio was founded in 2001 by Toby Revis as a single point of contact for designers and collectors who want a carpet made the way it should be — drawn by hand, dyed in small batches, knotted on a Tibetan loom in Nepal.

A note from the founder

I started this studio because I could not find the rug I wanted.

I had spent fifteen years in fine jewellery — gemology at Van Cleef, the Elsa Peretti collection at Tiffany — and I had been trained to look closely at one thing at a time. When I started buying rugs for my own homes and clients, what I kept finding was the opposite of close looking: dye lots that did not match the sample, rugs in stock pretending to be custom, weave counts that lied to themselves on the label.

So I went up the supply chain until I found the looms. I went to Kathmandu. I met the weaving masters. I learned to read a cartoon. I learned the difference between a Tibetan knot and a Persian one — and what each one will do to your light. Twenty‑five years later, that is still the studio: one room, one rug, one knot at a time.

— Toby Revis, founder

Philosophy

Direct to the source. Heirloom from day one.

We work without an intermediary. The conversation that begins on Madison Avenue is the same conversation that finishes at the loom in Kathmandu, and the carpet that arrives is the carpet that was drawn.

We are a small studio on purpose. We accept a limited number of commissions a year so that every project has Toby on it — from the first conversation to the final installation. The carpets we make are heirlooms from the day they are unrolled. They are designed to live in a family for three generations and to look more beautiful in the third than they did in the first.
