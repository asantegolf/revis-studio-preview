02 The studio

We make rugs the slow way. Hand‑spun wool, vegetable‑dyed in small batches, knotted one row at a time on traditional Tibetan looms — a method that has not been improved upon in five hundred years.

Revis Studio is a single point of contact for architects, interior designers and private collectors who want a carpet made the way it should be — direct from the loom, without the middlemen, without the warehouses, without compromise on color, scale, or proportion.

04 The process

12 — 26 weeks · concept to installation

From a conversation in our atelier to a finished carpet on your floor — one room, one rug, one knot at a time.

  1. Concept

    Inspiration & brief

    A conversation in the studio or on site. We work from a designer’s mood board, a swatch of a fabric, the color of a sky, a Klimt detail, the patina of a floor. Nothing is too abstract to begin with.

  2. Drawing

    Cartoon & scale

    A full‑scale technical drawing — the cartoon — is produced and refined. We mock up scale, repeat, and edge proportions in your space using elevations and on‑floor tape‑outs.

  3. Color

    Dye lab & sampling

    Wool yarns are batch‑dyed by hand using vegetable pigments or premium Swiss dyes. Sample knots — typically 12 × 12 inches — are sent for approval in daylight against the room’s palette.

  4. Loom

    Hand‑knotting in Nepal

    A team of master weavers near Kathmandu sets the warp and ties each knot by hand using the traditional Tibetan technique. A 9 × 12 carpet at 100 knots per square inch takes six to nine months on the loom.

  5. Finish

    Wash, stretch & carve

    After knotting, the rug is washed in spring water to bloom the lanolin, sun‑stretched, and — where the design calls for it — hand‑carved to relieve the pile.

  6. Install

    Delivery & presentation

    We oversee freight, customs, and white‑glove installation in your space — including pad, binding, and on‑site adjustment for the final fit.

05 Material

Why our rugs age beautifully

Our wool is shorn from sheep that graze the high plateaus of the Himalaya.

Long‑staple Tibetan wool carries an unusually high concentration of lanolin — a natural oil that makes the fibre resistant to soil, lustrous in daylight, and surprisingly springy under foot. A Revis carpet does not wear out. It develops a patina, the way good leather does.

09 Frequently asked

Common questions, plainly answered

Frequently asked questions

How long does a custom hand‑knotted rug take to make?

From the first conversation to installation, a typical Revis commission runs twelve to twenty‑six weeks. Design, cartoon and color approval take roughly four to six; hand‑knotting a 9 × 12 carpet at 100 knots per square inch in our Kathmandu loom takes another six to nine months on average; finishing and shipping account for the remainder.

What materials do you weave with?

Our base fibre is Tibetan long‑staple wool from sheep raised on the high plateaus of the Himalaya. Its high lanolin content gives a Revis carpet its lustre, soil resistance and characteristic spring underfoot. We also weave with hand‑reeled Chinese mulberry silk, Himalayan nettle (allo), and natural linen for accent fields. Dyes are either vegetable pigments — madder, indigo, walnut, pomegranate — or premium Swiss synthetic dyes when sun‑fastness is critical.

Do you work directly with interior designers and architects?

Yes. The studio was built for the trade. Designers, architects and decorators receive direct‑to‑source pricing, color and yarn consultation, full‑size sampling, and a single point of contact from the first sketch through to on‑site installation. To open a trade account, please write to toby@revisstudio.com.

What sizes and shapes can you produce?

Any. We have woven runners as narrow as 2 × 14 feet for Manhattan hallways and seamless fields as large as 20 × 30 feet for hotel lobbies. Round, oval, irregular, stepped and inset shapes are produced from your room dimensions; we will mock the rug on the floor in tape before the loom is set.

Where is the studio located, and can I visit?

The studio is at 225 East 74th Street, #6N, New York, NY 10021, on Manhattan’s Upper East Side. Visits are by appointment Monday through Friday — please call 212 987 8544 or write to toby@revisstudio.com.

How are Revis carpets different from ‘designer’ rugs at a showroom?

Revis is the maker, not a reseller. There is no middle showroom, no warehoused inventory, no minimum reorder run. Each carpet is drawn for one room, dyed for one palette, knotted to one set of measurements. The price is the cost of the carpet — not the cost of distribution.

How should a hand‑knotted wool rug be cared for?

Vacuum weekly without a beater bar. Rotate 180° once a year so light wear is even. Blot — never rub — spills with a clean white cloth. A Revis carpet does not need to be cleaned often; the lanolin in the wool repels soil. When a deep clean is wanted, we recommend a hand‑washer that specialises in Tibetan and Persian rugs; we are happy to make a referral in your city.
